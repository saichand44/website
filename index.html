About Me

I am currently pursuing my Master's degree in Robotics at the University of Pennsylvania. I am interested in the fields of Computer Vision and Perception. My experience spans diverse areas within these fields, working on projects related to Structure from Motion (SfM), Optical Flow, Camera Pose Estimation, Epipolar Geometry, Projective Geometry, NeRFs and extracting key features in images.

Previously, I worked at AIRBUS as a Multibody Simulation specialist, with a focus on building MBS models using script-based modeling techniques, comparing results between MBS models and physical test campaigns. I had the privilege of contributing to projects involving prominent aircraft such as the A350, A321 and next-generation aircraft.

I am an ardent follower of Formula 1 and Soccer. Throughout my undergraduate studies at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, I spent a great deal of time actively contributing to the Formula Student racecar project at TeamKART, where I led the Vehicle Dynamics team.

Projects

Augmented Reality

Seamless integration of virtual objects into the real-world scene where users can choose the initial placement of virtual objects. As the camera moves and/or rotates, the position and orientation of virtual objects dynamically update. The camera pose in every frame is estimated using AprilTags and P3P / PnP algorithms

3D-Reconstruction

Generating a point cloud that resembles the 3D geometry of the object using a two-view stereo algorithm. This algorithm takes in pixels from two images at a time and converts them to 3D points. Keypoint correspondences are computed using a disparity map between the given pair of 2D images

Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs)

Synthesizing novel views of a given scene using a fully-connected neural network trained on set of images with known camera poses. The network takes in spatial location and viewing direction and outputs the volume density and view-dependent emitted radiance at that spatial location

Object Detection and Tracking

Detecting moving objects in a video sequence using YOLOv3. The YOLOv3 model was trained using COCO dataset. Object tracking is implemented using DeepSORT. It uses feature descriptors from the FaceNet model to associate detections of the same object across different time frames

Object Detection

Detecting football players and football in a video clip using OpenCV library and traditional image processing techniques such as masking, morphological transformations, edge detection, Hough transformations. Used homography matrix to project players, football to bird's eye view.

Pick and Place Challenge

Controlling a 7 DOF Franka-Emika Panda robot using a Positional Inverse Kinematics approach. Objective is to stack blocks by picking them from static and moving platforms. Our team successfully stacked 7 blocks (4 static and 3 dynamic) and stood second out of the 20 teams participated!
